## Prompt

I need a cover letter for a defense industry embedded/test engineering 
position. Follow these steps in order:

Step 1: Analyze my background — Java/Spring Boot backend (microservices, 
Docker, TDD practices) and newly acquired C++ skills (ring buffer, 
CHIP-8 emulator project). Identify which of these is most relevant to 
an embedded/test engineering role and why.

Step 2: Explain my transition narrative — how backend engineering 
discipline (testing rigor, systems thinking) translates into embedded/
test engineering, framing the C++ learning as a deliberate, motivated 
move rather than a random skill addition.

Step 3: Select ONE concrete project (ring buffer or CHIP-8 emulator) 
and describe the specific technical challenge it involved, to serve as 
proof of hands-on capability — not just a list of buzzwords.

Step 4: Write the cover letter using the above reasoning, in a formal, 
precise tone appropriate for the defense industry (avoid casual 
enthusiasm language like "passionate" or "fast learner").

Step 5: End with a concise closing paragraph that requests an interview, 
without generic filler phrases.

I am seeking to apply this combination of backend-honed testing rigor and emerging low-level systems knowledge to a role where reliability is non-negotiable. I would welcome the opportunity to discuss how this background fits your team's needs.
Sincerely,
