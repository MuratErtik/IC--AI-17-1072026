Document 3: Why Enterprises Adopted RAG (2022-2024 Timeline)

While RAG originated as an academic technique in 2020, its widespread 
enterprise adoption is closely tied to the release of ChatGPT in 
November 2022 and the subsequent explosion of enterprise interest in 
deploying LLMs on internal company data.

Timeline of enterprise-facing RAG tooling:
- Early 2023: LangChain and LlamaIndex (then GPT Index) emerged as 
  open-source frameworks that made it easy for developers to build 
  RAG pipelines connecting LLMs to custom document stores.
- Mid 2023: Major cloud providers began offering managed RAG services 
  — for example, Azure AI Search with "Add your data" for Azure OpenAI, 
  and Amazon Bedrock Knowledge Bases.
- 2024: RAG became a standard architecture pattern for enterprise 
  chatbots, internal knowledge assistants, and customer support tools, 
  often combined with citation/source-attribution features so that 
  responses could be traced back to specific source documents.

Regarding source attribution: production RAG systems typically return 
not just the generated answer but also metadata about which document 
chunks were retrieved and used, allowing the system (or the user) to 
verify claims against the original source. This directly addresses 
the hallucination risk of ungrounded LLM responses, since answers can 
be checked against retrievable, citable source material rather than 
relying solely on the model's internal (and sometimes outdated or 
incorrect) parametric knowledge.

Source: Industry survey compilation, "State of Enterprise RAG 
Adoption 2023-2024."
